Common Questions

Buyer FAQs

A general rule of thumb is that your monthly housing payment should not exceed 28-30% of your gross monthly income. However, this depends on your debts, down payment, interest rate, and local market conditions. I recommend getting pre-approved by a lender to know your exact budget.
The typical process includes: 1) Getting pre-approved for a mortgage, 2) House hunting and viewing properties, 3) Making an offer, 4) Negotiating terms, 5) Home inspection, 6) Appraisal, 7) Final walkthrough, and 8) Closing. I guide my clients through each step to make it as smooth as possible.
Down payment requirements vary by loan type. Conventional loans typically require 5-20%, FHA loans as little as 3.5%, and VA loans may require 0% for eligible veterans. A larger down payment usually means a lower monthly payment and may help you avoid private mortgage insurance (PMI).
Closing costs typically range from 2-5% of the home's purchase price and include fees for the lender, title company, appraiser, and other services. As a buyer, some of these costs may be negotiable. I'll help you understand and potentially reduce your closing costs during negotiations.
Absolutely. A home inspection is one of the most important steps in the buying process. It can reveal hidden issues with the property's structure, electrical, plumbing, roof, and more. The inspection findings can be used to negotiate repairs or a price reduction with the seller.
When you find a home you love, I'll prepare a competitive offer based on market analysis. The seller can accept, reject, or counter your offer. Negotiations may go back and forth on price, contingencies, closing date, and included items. I advocate for your interests at every step.
Earnest money is a good-faith deposit that shows the seller you're serious about purchasing. It's typically 1-3% of the purchase price and is held in escrow until closing. The money is applied toward your down payment or closing costs. If the deal falls through due to a covered contingency, you typically get it back.
